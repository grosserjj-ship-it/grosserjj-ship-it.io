What Clearwater Paper Actually Does

Clearwater Paper is a manufacturer of SBS (solid bleached sulfate) paperboard â€” the premium, high-brightness coated paperboard used for folding cartons, liquid packaging, cups, plates, and food-contact packaging. Following the sale of its consumer tissue business (completed 2024) and the acquisition of the Augusta, Georgia mill from Pixelle, Clearwater is now a pure-play SBS paperboard company with two mills: Spokane, WA and Augusta, GA.

SBS is a commodity with strong quality differentiation. It's used for Starbucks cups, pharmaceutical packaging, premium food cartons, and single-serving beverage containers. The market is dominated by a handful of integrated manufacturers. CLW, at $285M market cap, is the smallest public pure-play in the space â€” and currently the most beaten-down.

Understanding the SBS Paperboard Cycle

SBS paperboard is a cyclical commodity. Pricing follows capacity utilization. When utilization is high (>94%), producers have pricing power and margins expand. When capacity overhang exists â€” driven by new mill builds or imports â€” pricing weakens and margins compress. The current trough (2024â€“2025) was driven by post-COVID demand normalization, excess European imports benefiting from a strong dollar, and the capacity addition from Pixelle's Augusta mill (which CLW then acquired, removing it from the competitive supply picture).

RISI (the industry's primary data provider) is forecasting 350,000 tons of net capacity reduction in H1 2026 â€” roughly 8â€“10% of the addressable market. European imports are declining. Demand from the beverage and food-contact segments is recovering. This is the setup for a classic cyclical recovery. CLW is positioned with two mills, $450M+ liquidity, and FY26 guidance that implies the bottom has been seen.
